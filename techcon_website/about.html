TechCon's History

The Evolution of TechCon: From Local Meetups to Continental Hub

TechCon, a leading annual technology conference in Sandton, South Africa, began as informal "Joburg Tech Meetups" around 2008. These initial gatherings, fueled by passionate entrepreneurs and developers, aimed to connect the burgeoning local tech community. The success of these meetups led to the first official TechCon in 2011, held in a modest conference center in Johannesburg. This inaugural event laid the groundwork for the conference's future growth and influence.

In the subsequent years, TechCon experienced significant expansion, attracting a growing number of attendees, including investors and international experts. The conference program broadened, incorporating diverse topics like Fintech, e-commerce, and mobile technologies, reflecting the evolving African tech landscape. As it gained prominence, TechCon began drawing significant sponsorships, further enhancing its scale and quality. The strategic move to the world-class facilities in Sandton marked a pivotal moment, allowing for increased attendance and expanded exhibition spaces.

TechCon's Mission

TechCon's mission is to be the leading platform for driving technological advancement, collaboration, and innovation across Africa. Its core principles include empowerment, collaboration, inspiration, growth, and inclusivity, aiming to equip individuals and businesses, foster partnerships, showcase groundbreaking technologies, contribute to economic development, and create a welcoming environment for all. The conference seeks to unlock Africa's potential by uniting tech minds and promising technologies to shape a digitally empowered future.

These speakers represent the diverse expertise and impactful contributions that have shaped TechCon and advanced technological progress in Africa.
